Le 20 Septembre, l’Afrique du Sud passera le relais \u00e0 la France pour la pr\u00e9sidence du Partenariat pour un Gouvernement Ouvert<\/a> \u00e0 l’occasion d’une c\u00e9r\u00e9monie rassemblant Chefs d’Etat et soci\u00e9t\u00e9 civile en marge de l’Assembl\u00e9e G\u00e9n\u00e9rale des Nations-Unies<\/strong><\/a>,<\/strong> qui c\u00e9l\u00e8brera \u00e9galement les cinq ans du Partenariat.<\/p>\n

Le lendemain, agents de l\u2019administration fran\u00e7aise et am\u00e9ricaine, repr\u00e9sentants de la soci\u00e9t\u00e9 civile, entreprises, start-ups et membres de la communaut\u00e9 civic tech<\/em> seront mobilis\u00e9s pour contribuer \u00e0 la mise en \u0153uvre concr\u00e8te des principes et engagements du gouvernement ouvert. Cette bo\u00eete \u00e0 outils vise \u00e0 faciliter la mise en \u0153uvre des engagements pris dans le cadre du PGO, en r\u00e9f\u00e9ren\u00e7ant les solutions disponibles et en documentant leurs usages.<\/p>\n

L’\u00e9v\u00e9nement new-yorkais, organis\u00e9 de 9h \u00e0 20h30 <\/strong>(voir le programme),<\/a> sera l’occasion de pr\u00e9senter la bo\u00eete \u00e0 outils et de recueillir les contributions de l’administration am\u00e9ricaine et de l’\u00e9cosyst\u00e8me local.<\/strong> Cr\u00e9e en 2015, le Civic Hall new-yorkais est un espace ouvert et contributif d\u00e9di\u00e9 aux innovations citoyennes, o\u00f9 innovateurs, entrepreneurs sociaux, agents publics, journalistes et universitaires \u00e9changent et d\u00e9veloppent conjointement des projets d’int\u00e9r\u00eat g\u00e9n\u00e9ral.<\/p>\n

  • La d\u00e9marche et les sprints Toolbox<\/em><\/strong><\/span><\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    <\/strong><\/p>\n

    Cet \u00e9v\u00e9nement fait partie d\u2019une s\u00e9rie de sprints et d’\u00e9v\u00e9nements contributif<\/strong>s qui m\u00e8neront au lancement de la bo\u00eete \u00e0 outils lors du Sommet Mondial du PGO<\/a> qui aura lieu \u00e0 Paris les 7, 8 et 9 d\u00e9cembre 2016.<\/p>\n

    Cette plateforme fournira un catalogue agr\u00e9ment\u00e9 de cas d’usage de solutions d’ \u00ab\u00a0open government<\/em>\u00a0\u00bb : portails d\u2019open data, plateformes de consultation citoyenne, outils de suivi et d\u2019\u00e9laboration collaborative de la loi, forums, plateformes d\u2019\u00e9valuation des plans d\u2019action nationaux\u2026<\/p>\n

    Lors de ces sprints, les participants renseignent leurs outils via une interface de saisie, documentent et actualisent les solutions recens\u00e9es, et am\u00e9liorent de fa\u00e7on collaborative les logiciels et services num\u00e9riques<\/strong>. Les pr\u00e9c\u00e9dents \u00e9v\u00e9nements ont d\u00e9j\u00e0 permis de moissonner des catalogues existants, d\u2019imaginer des r\u00e9utilisations de la base de donn\u00e9es comme civictech.lol<\/a>, de travailler sur le packaging des logiciels pour en faciliter l\u2019installation automatique et de faire un benchmark des UI\/UX<\/a> les plus adapt\u00e9es \u00e0 la plateforme.<\/p>\n

    Ces r\u00e9unions sont aussi l\u2019occasion de collaborer et de mutualiser des efforts avec des initiatives internationales similaires<\/strong> telles que le wiki <\/a>de Nuit Debout, la plateforme Civic Stack<\/a> de Democracia en Red et Asuntos del Sur, la plateforme ParticipateDB<\/a> d\u2019Intellectics, le Civic Graph<\/a> de Microsoft, la plateforme Dem.tools<\/a> du NDI ou encore la cartographie de la technologie civique en France<\/a> propos\u00e9e par le Secretariat d\u2019Etat charg\u00e9 du num\u00e9rique, \u00e0 travers le partage de donn\u00e9es ou la r\u00e9flexion commune sur la taxonomie des initiatives et des acteurs de la civic tech.<\/p>\n

      (images : Civic Hall, Etalab)<\/em>[:en]On September 21, Etalab and Civic Hall are co-organizing a hackathon in New York<\/a> to contribute to the open government toolbox<\/a>.<\/strong><\/p>\n

      On September 20, South Africa will hand over the Open Government Partnership (OGP) Presidency to France during a ceremony gathering Heads of State and civil society representatives that will be held during the General Assembly of the United Nations<\/strong>.<\/p>\n

      The next day, public officials from the French and American governments, civil society representatives, private companies, start-ups, and members of the civic tech community will gather to contribute to the concrete implementation of open government principles and commitments. The OGP Toolbox is intended to facilitate the implementation of national commitments made by governments in their action plans. It will be based on a catalogue referencing software and online services, including examples of use cases.<\/p>\n

      The hackathon will take place from 9 am to 8:30 pm<\/strong> (see program<\/a>) and will provide the opportunity to present the OGP Toolbox and to collect contributions from the American government and the local ecosystem<\/strong>. Created in 2015, Civic Hall is a new co-working space for civic tech where innovators, social entrepreneurs, government employees, journalists and academics exchange views and build tools to solve problems together.<\/p>\n

      • OGP Toolbox events
        \n<\/strong><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        This hackathon is part of a series of sprints and workshops organized in preparation of the launch of the OGP Toolbox that will take place during the OGP 2016 Global Summit<\/a> in Paris (7, 8 and 9 December).<\/p>\n

        This toolbox will be based on a catalogue including examples of use cases and technical criteria: open data portals, public consultation platforms, tools for monitoring and co-creating the law, discussion forums, online platforms to monitor the implementation of national action plans\u2026<\/p>\n

        During these sprints, participants are able to add their tools to the catalogue via a crowdsourcing interface, document and update indexed solutions, and work collaboratively to improve software and online services<\/strong>. At previous events, participants harvested existing catalogues, imagined reuses for the database such as civictech.lol<\/a>, packaged software to facilitate automatic installation, and set a benchmark of UI\/UX<\/a> applicable to the catalogue.<\/p>\n

        These events also constitute an opportunity to collaborate and coordinate efforts with similar international initiatives, such as Nuit Debout\u2019s wiki<\/a>, Democracia en Red and Asuntos del Sur\u2019s Civic Stack<\/a> platform, Microsoft\u2019s Civic Graph<\/a>, NDI\u2019s Dem.tools<\/a> or the French civic technology cartography<\/a> made by the Minister of State for Digital Affairs, through data sharing or common reflection on civic tech taxonomy.<\/p>\n
